Frequently Asked Questions

What genre is Skrillex?

Skrillex is primarily known for his work in the electronic dance music (EDM) genre, with a particular focus on dubstep. However, his music also incorporates elements of various other genres including electro house, post-hardcore, and trap. Skrillex, whose real name is Sonny John Moore, has been influential in popularizing dubstep and has won multiple Grammy Awards for his contributions to electronic music.

Is it illegal to sample drum breaks?

The legality of sampling drum breaks depends on how the sample is used and whether permission has been obtained. If a drum break from an existing recording is sampled without clearance or permission from the copyright holder, it could be considered copyright infringement, which is illegal. However, if the sample is cleared, meaning the user has obtained permission or a license to use the drum break, then it is legal. Additionally, some drum breaks may be in the public domain or offered under licenses that allow for their use without direct permission, but these cases are less common in commercial music production.

When did dubstep peak?

Dubstep, a genre of electronic dance music, experienced its peak in popularity from the late 2000s to the early 2010s. Originating in South London in the late 1990s, it gained significant attention and a dedicated following in the UK before spreading to the US and other countries. Artists like Skrillex, Rusko, and Benga, along with labels like Tempa, played pivotal roles in bringing dubstep to mainstream audiences. The genre's presence was notably prominent at music festivals, in clubs, and across various media platforms during this period.

How much does splice pay?

Splice, a platform known for its sample library, plugins, and digital audio workstation (DAW) integrations, compensates artists and creators through a revenue-sharing model. The specific amount Splice pays to individual creators depends on the popularity and usage of their samples or products within the platform. Creators earn a share of the revenue generated from subscriptions or purchases of their content, but the exact figures can vary widely based on factors such as the number of downloads and the overall demand for their contributions.

What is modern dubstep called?

Modern dubstep, often referred to as "brostep" in the United States, is a subgenre of dubstep that emerged in the late 2000s and early 2010s. It is characterized by its aggressive style, featuring high energy levels, heavy use of wobble bass, and complex, syncopated rhythms. Artists like Skrillex, Excision, and Knife Party are among the most notable figures in this subgenre, having played a significant role in popularizing the sound and bringing it to a broader audience. Brostep has significantly influenced the direction of electronic music, blending elements of dubstep with other genres to create a distinct, energetic sound.
