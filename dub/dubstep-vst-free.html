Frequently Asked Questions

What the f is dubstep?

Dubstep is a genre of electronic dance music that originated in South London in the late 1990s. It is characterized by its sparse, syncopated rhythmic patterns with prominent sub-bass frequencies, and the occasional use of reggae-like drum patterns. The genre gained significant popularity in the early to mid-2000s and has influenced many other genres of music. Dubstep is known for its heavy use of wobble bass, where an instrument's volume or pitch is manipulated, creating a distinctive sound.

Why did Skrillex disappear?

Skrillex, the renowned electronic music producer and DJ, didn't exactly disappear but rather took a step back from the limelight to focus on various aspects of his career and personal growth. After achieving massive success and a hectic touring schedule, he decided to take some time off to work on new music, explore different creative projects, and collaborate with other artists in a more behind-the-scenes capacity. This period of relative quietude was also a time for him to recharge and seek inspiration for future works. Despite being less visible in the mainstream media, Skrillex continued to influence the music industry through his production work and occasional performances.

Do small artists get sued for sampling?

Yes, small artists can get sued for sampling if they use someone else's copyrighted material without permission. Regardless of the artist's size or the extent of their audience, copyright laws apply universally. However, lawsuits are more likely when the sampled work gains significant attention or commercial success, which might draw the original copyright holder's attention. It's important for all artists to either clear samples through proper licensing or use royalty-free or public domain samples to avoid legal issues.

What is a drum sample pack?

A drum sample pack is a collection of drum sounds or beats that producers and musicians can use to create music. These packs typically include a variety of drum hits, loops, and rhythms, recorded from different types of drums and percussion instruments. They are designed to be easily integrated into digital audio workstations (DAWs) to enhance music production, allowing users to experiment with diverse drum sounds without needing to record live drums. Drum sample packs are popular in genres ranging from hip-hop to electronic music, providing a versatile tool for creativity and production.
