Frequently Asked Questions

What did Skrillex invent?

Skrillex, born Sonny John Moore, is widely credited with popularizing the electronic dance music (EDM) genre known as dubstep in the United States and beyond. While he did not invent dubstep itself�a genre that originated in South London in the late 1990s�his unique approach and production style significantly influenced its evolution and brought it into the mainstream. Skrillex's aggressive and distinct blend of electronic music, characterized by its heavy use of bass and intricate, chopped-up samples, has had a lasting impact on the EDM scene and has inspired countless artists across various music genres.

What is the hardest drum beat?

The concept of the "hardest drum beat" is subjective and varies among drummers, depending on their skill level, experience, and style of music. However, some complex rhythms are widely recognized for their difficulty. For example, the "Blast Beat," commonly used in extreme metal genres, requires rapid, synchronized hits on the snare and bass drum, challenging even seasoned drummers. Another example is the "Purdie Shuffle," a groove that involves a nuanced, intricate pattern, demanding a high level of coordination and touch. These beats, among others, are often cited as challenging due to their speed, complexity, and the endurance they require.

Why is dubstep called 140?

Dubstep is often referred to as "140" because of its characteristic tempo, which typically hovers around 140 beats per minute (BPM). This genre of electronic dance music emerged in the late 1990s and early 2000s in South London, evolving from the roots of UK garage, drum and bass, and reggae. The tempo of 140 BPM is a defining feature that helps distinguish dubstep from other genres, providing a rhythmic framework for its deep basslines, reverberant drum patterns, and occasional vocals. This specific tempo contributes to the unique feel and energy of dubstep tracks, making "140" a shorthand reference to the genre itself among fans and producers.

Is it illegal to sample drum breaks?

The legality of sampling drum breaks depends on how the sample is used and whether permission has been obtained from the original copyright holder. If a drum break from a copyrighted work is sampled without authorization, it could be considered copyright infringement, which is illegal. However, if the sample is cleared with the copyright owner through licensing or falls under fair use (which is determined on a case-by-case basis and might be difficult to prove), then it would not be illegal. It's important for creators to understand copyright laws and seek proper clearance when sampling copyrighted material to avoid legal issues.

Who gets paid for a sample?

When a sample of music, sound, or any other form of media is used in a new work, the original creator or copyright holder of the sampled material typically gets paid. This payment comes in the form of licensing fees or royalties, which are negotiated before the sample is used. The specifics of the payment can vary greatly depending on the popularity of the sample, the way it's being used, and the agreement between the parties involved. In some cases, the artist using the sample might also agree to give a percentage of their song's earnings to the original creator.
