Frequently Asked Questions

Is Skrillex a dubstep or brostep?

Skrillex is often associated with both dubstep and brostep, genres that have evolved within electronic dance music. Dubstep originated in the late 1990s in the UK, characterized by its sparse rhythms and emphasis on bass and sub-bass. Brostep, a subgenre of dubstep that gained popularity in the early 2010s, especially in the United States, is known for its aggressive style and complex basslines. Skrillex, with his signature sound that includes high energy drops and growling basslines, is frequently credited with popularizing brostep, though his music incorporates elements from various genres, including traditional dubstep.

Why is dubstep 140 and not 70?

Dubstep is traditionally set at a tempo of around 140 beats per minute (BPM) rather than 70 BPM because this tempo allows for a more energetic and intense listening experience, which is a hallmark of the genre. The half-time rhythm commonly used in dubstep makes the music feel slower than it actually is, giving it a heavy, powerful feel despite the faster tempo. This characteristic tempo supports the genre's complex basslines and syncopated rhythms, creating a unique sound that wouldn't be as impactful at a slower BPM.

Who is the most famous dubstep DJ?

The most famous dubstep DJ is often considered to be Skrillex, born Sonny John Moore. Skrillex is credited with bringing dubstep and its related genres into the mainstream music scene. His energetic performances, innovative production techniques, and multiple Grammy Awards have solidified his position as a leading figure in the electronic music industry. Skrillex's influence extends beyond dubstep, impacting various genres and inspiring countless artists around the world.

Why is melodic dubstep so good?

Melodic dubstep is often considered so good because it masterfully combines the heavy, gritty bass and beats characteristic of traditional dubstep with melodious harmonies and emotional chord progressions. This fusion creates a powerful, immersive listening experience that can evoke a wide range of emotions, from euphoria to introspection. The contrast between the aggressive dubstep elements and the soothing melodies also adds a dynamic complexity to the music, making it appealing to a broader audience. Moreover, the melodic elements make the genre more accessible and enjoyable to those who might not be fans of the heavier aspects of electronic music.
