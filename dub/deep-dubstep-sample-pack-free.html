What's the difference between dubstep and brostep?

Dubstep and brostep are both subgenres of electronic dance music, but they have distinct characteristics. Dubstep, originating in the late 1990s in the UK, is known for its sparse rhythms, dark atmosphere, and emphasis on bass and sub-bass frequencies, often featuring a tempo around 140 beats per minute. Brostep, a term that emerged in the early 2010s, is a subgenre of dubstep that originated in the United States and is characterized by a more aggressive sound, with a focus on mid-range frequencies, complex bass wobbles, and metallic sounds. While dubstep maintains a more underground vibe, brostep is often associated with the American EDM scene and has a more mainstream appeal.
