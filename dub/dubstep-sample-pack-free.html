Why is 30 samples enough?

In statistics, the number of 30 samples is often considered enough due to the Central Limit Theorem, which states that the distribution of the sample means will approximate a normal distribution as the sample size becomes larger, typically reaching a good approximation with as few as 30 samples. This threshold allows for meaningful analysis and inference about the population from which the samples are drawn, assuming the samples are independent and identically distributed. Additionally, a sample size of 30 strikes a balance between practicality and the precision of estimates, making it a widely accepted minimum for many types of statistical analyses.

What are you legally allowed to sample?

Legally, you are allowed to sample copyrighted material under certain conditions that fall under the doctrine of fair use. This includes using small portions of a work for purposes such as criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, or research. However, the legality of sampling also depends on factors like the amount of the work used, the nature of the copyrighted work, the effect on the market value of the original work, and the purpose of the use.
