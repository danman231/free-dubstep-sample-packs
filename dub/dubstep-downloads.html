What is the most used drum break?

The most used drum break is the "Amen Break," originating from the song "Amen, Brother" by The Winstons, a B-side released in 1969. This six-second drum solo has been extensively sampled and looped in countless tracks across various music genres, particularly in hip hop, drum and bass, and jungle music. Its popularity stems from its compelling rhythm and the ease with which it can be looped and mixed into other tracks. What BPM is dubstep at?

Dubstep typically has a tempo of around 140 beats per minute (BPM). However, it's characterized by a half-time rhythm, which means that while the overall BPM is 140, the feel of the music might seem like it's at 70 BPM due to the spacing of the beats and bass hits. This slower, more spaced-out rhythm pattern is a defining characteristic of the dubstep genre, giving it its distinctive heavy and syncopated bass sound.
