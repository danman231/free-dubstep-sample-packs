Frequently Asked Questions

What BPM is most dubstep?

Most dubstep music typically has a tempo around 140 beats per minute (BPM). This tempo is a defining characteristic of the genre, providing a slow, heavy beat that allows for the spacious basslines and unique rhythmic patterns dubstep is known for. However, it's not uncommon to find tracks that play around this tempo, sometimes going lower to create a more relaxed vibe or slightly higher to inject more energy into the track.

How much of a song can you legally sample?

The legality of sampling a song varies by country and can depend on the length, nature of the use, and whether the sample is recognizable. In the United States, there is no specific rule about how much of a song you can sample without permission; even a short sample may require clearance if it is identifiable. The key factor is whether the sample constitutes a substantial portion of the original work in both qualitative and quantitative measures. Therefore, to legally sample a song, it is often safest to obtain permission from the copyright holder to avoid potential infringement issues.

Is it legal to use 10 seconds of a song?

The legality of using 10 seconds of a song depends on various factors, including the purpose of use, the country's copyright laws, and whether the use falls under fair use or fair dealing exceptions. In some cases, using a short clip of a song for purposes such as criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, or research may be considered fair use. However, for commercial purposes or to create a new piece of work, even a short segment like 10 seconds may require permission or a license from the copyright holder. It's important to consult the specific copyright laws applicable in your jurisdiction or seek legal advice.
