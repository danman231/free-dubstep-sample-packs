Frequently Asked Questions

Can you sample dead artists?

Yes, you can sample music from dead artists, but it's important to understand that the legality of doing so depends on copyright laws, which do not expire upon the artist's death. Copyrights in music are typically held for 70 years after the death of the last surviving author (in many jurisdictions). Therefore, to legally sample music from a deceased artist, you generally need to obtain permission from the copyright holder, which could be the artist's estate, a record label, or another entity that has acquired the rights. Failure to obtain the necessary permissions can result in legal action, including lawsuits for copyright infringement.

Do rappers get permission to sample?

Rappers, like other musicians, typically need to obtain permission to sample someone else's music in their tracks. This process involves clearing the sample, which means getting approval from the copyright holders of the original song, which could include the songwriter, performer, and record label. Failure to get proper clearance can lead to legal issues, including copyright infringement lawsuits. However, some artists choose to use samples without permission, either hoping not to be noticed or considering the potential legal battle worth the risk.

What song is baddest of them all sampling?

The song "Baddest of Them All" samples the track "Flowers" by Sweet Female Attitude, which is a UK garage song released in the year 2000. The sampled melody and elements from "Flowers" are integrated into "Baddest of Them All," giving it a nostalgic yet fresh feel. This combination of old and new has contributed to the song's popularity on various social media platforms and music charts.

What the f is dubstep?

Dubstep is a genre of electronic dance music that originated in South London in the late 1990s. It is characterized by its sparse, syncopated rhythmic patterns with prominent sub-bass frequencies, and the occasional use of reggae-like drum patterns. The genre gained significant popularity in the early to mid-2000s and has influenced many other genres of music. Dubstep is known for its heavy use of wobble bass, where an instrument's volume or pitch is manipulated, creating a distinctive sound.

Why did Skrillex disappear?

Skrillex, the renowned electronic music producer and DJ, didn't exactly disappear but rather took a step back from the limelight to focus on various aspects of his career and personal growth. After achieving massive success and a hectic touring schedule, he decided to take some time off to work on new music, explore different creative projects, and collaborate with other artists in a more behind-the-scenes capacity. This period of relative quietude was also a time for him to recharge and seek inspiration for future works. Despite being less visible in the mainstream media, Skrillex continued to influence the music industry through his production work and occasional performances.
