Frequently Asked Questions

Is sample music legal?

Sample music can be legal, but it depends on how it is used. If a musician or producer wants to use a sample from another artist's work, they typically need to obtain permission from the copyright holder and may need to pay for a license. Without obtaining the necessary rights, using a sample could be considered copyright infringement, which is illegal. However, some uses of samples may fall under fair use, depending on factors like the amount of the sample used and its effect on the market value of the original work.

What genre is Skrillex?

Skrillex is primarily known for his work in the electronic dance music (EDM) genre, with a particular focus on dubstep. However, his music also incorporates elements of various other genres including electro house, post-hardcore, and trap. Skrillex, whose real name is Sonny John Moore, has been influential in popularizing dubstep and has won multiple Grammy Awards for his contributions to electronic music.

Is it illegal to sample drum breaks?

The legality of sampling drum breaks depends on how the sample is used and whether permission has been obtained. If a drum break from an existing recording is sampled without clearance or permission from the copyright holder, it could be considered copyright infringement, which is illegal. However, if the sample is cleared, meaning the user has obtained permission or a license to use the drum break, then it is legal. Additionally, some drum breaks may be in the public domain or offered under licenses that allow for their use without direct permission, but these cases are less common in commercial music production.
